    6/10/2020

    Dear Patients,

    Our community has been through a lot over the last few months, and all of us are looking forward to resuming our normal habits and routines. While many things have changed, one thing has remained the same: our commitment to your safety.

    Infection control has always been a top priority for our practice, and you'll soon see some changes meant to enhance our already stringent measures in that area. We'd like to tell you about some of the procedures we follow in our practice to keep patients and staff safe.

    Our office follows infection control recommendations made by the American Dental Association (ADA), the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and OSHA. We follow the activities of these agencies so that we are up-to-date on any new rulings or guidance that may be issued. We do this to make sure that our infection control procedures are current and adhere to each agency’s recommendations.

    In the interest of protecting our patients and staff, we ask that you note the following changes in procedure:

    • Our office will communicate with you before your appointment to ask some screening questions. Upon arriving at the office, you'll be asked these questions again and your temperature will be taken.

    • We will have hand sanitizer as you enter the building and throughout the reception area. Hand washing can be done in the bathroom and treatment rooms as well.

    • We will not have magazines, children’s toys and other such materials available in the waiting area since these items are difficult to disinfect.


    Appointments will be managed to allow for social distancing between patients. That might mean that you’re offered fewer options for scheduling your appointment. We will do our best to space out our scheduled appointments to reduce both the number of patients in the waiting room and each patient's waiting time.

    We are happy to answer any questions you may have about the steps we have taken to keep you, and every patient, safe and comfortable in our practice.

    Sincerely,

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What is your billing policy?

    We don't participate with any particular insurance plans, but rather have one fee for all patients. However, if you have insurance we will gladly process their claim for any dental work. Generally, we prefer cash, check, Mastercard, or Visa as methods of payment at time of service. We also offer payment plans to our regular, trusted patients.

    What's the difference between a cap and a crown?

    Nothing -- they mean the same thing.

    A crown is recommended when more than half of the tooth structure is either gone or filled. Other reasons for getting a crown include having a root canal or symptoms of Cracked Tooth Syndrome (i.e. cold sensitivity and sharp pain upon biting down).

    Is bleaching safe?

    Yes ... if done as recommended.

    Bleaching can burn tissue if not applied exclusively to the enamel.

    Why do I need x-rays?

    Sometimes we can't detect problems within the teeth/bones themselves just by looking at their outsides -- x-rays provide us with that extra internal perspective. Children and the elderly actually develop decay and problems more readily due to changes in diet, hormones, and saliva chemistry. X-rays also allow us to assess the soundness of existing restorations.

    Will the shots hurt?

    We're told consistently that we give a 'gentle' shot; we also use a topical anesthesia before the injection to minimize the pain.
